Acanthopholis
a-KAN-tho-FOLE-is
[Cretaceous Europe]
Prickly Scale
It was an 18-foot long animal with an armor of rows of oval plates made from bone set in the skin, and sharp spikes along the middle of its back.
Acanthopholis was a herbivore. It lived in the Cretaceous period and inhabited Europe. Its fossils have been found in places such as England (United Kingdom).
Albertosaurus
al-BERT-oh-SAW-rus
[Cretaceous Americas]
Alberta Reptile
(Sometimes called Gorgosaurus) It lived right at the end of the age of dinosaurs. It was smaller than TYRANOSAURUS at about 26 feet long, but was still a fearsome beast.
Albertosaurus was a carnivore. It lived in the Cretaceous period and inhabited North America. Its fossils have been found in places such as Missouri, Nebraska and Montana.
Allosaurus
AL-oh-SAW-rus
[Jurassic Americas]
Different Reptile
This dinosaur was the main meat-eating dinosaur in North America in the late Jurrasic period. It was about 30-feet long. The Allosaurus was different from other meat-eating dinosaurs in the shapre of its skull, which has a ridge along the top that ran from between the eyes to the tip of its snout.
Allosaurus was a carnivore. It lived in the Jurassic period and inhabited Africa, Europe and North America. Its fossils have been found in places such as Utah, Colorado and Texas.
B
Barosaurus
BARR-oh-SAW-rus
[Jurassic Africa, Americas]
Heavy Reptile
This dinosaur grew uo to 90-feet long. The powerful neck vertebrae of this animal measures over a yard long. If it reared up on its hind legs, it could use its long neck to reach the leaves off the highest trees.
Barosaurus was a herbivore. It lived in the Jurassic period and inhabited North America. Its fossils have been found in places such as Utah, Texas and South Dakota.
